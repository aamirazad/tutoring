# Macbeth

## Act 1

### Scene 1

The poem starts with the setting of a storm. Three witches ask when they will meet again, “when the battle’s lost and won.” Likely foreshadowing the end of the play to set up for a full circle moment.

### Scene 2

**King Duncan** and his sons **Malcolm**, **Donalbain**, **Lennox**, and attendance meet a bleeding **captain**.
> But all’s too weak;
> For brave Macbeth (well he deserves that name) (16)

Introducing Macbeth as a very strong solder, the captain explains that **Macdonwald** was "merciless" but that brave Macbeth had killed him. Macbeth can only do so much against all the Irish soldiers, though, and the soldier's morale is low after putting their all in the battle, they need a break. (40).

The thane of **Ross** and **Angus** enter and tell that where Norwegian flags are all over, Macbeth "point against point" confronted them and "victory fell on us" (55).
> What he hath lost, noble Macbeth hath won. (67)

Macbeth tells the thane of Cowdor was a traitor so Duncan proclaims Macbeth to take his title.

### Scene 3

Back to the witches, who seem almost board. But with their immense power, they find enjoyment in controlling the winds to hurt sailors in the sea. (24).

The witches all hail Macbeth the thane of "Glamis" "Cowdor" and "king hereafter". (51).

> Thou shalt get kings, though thou be none. (69).

Speaking in riddle's the ghosts proclaim this prophecy. Macbeth understands he is the thane of Glamis but is confused of Cowdor, possible foreshadowing as the ghosts can probably see into the future. They leave when questioned.

Ross and Angus reveal to Macbeth that he is now the thane of Cawdor and that the previous man is sentenced to death. Macbeth is now fully invested in this prophecy as it holds some legitimacy since part of it came true. He is so over his own head that he considers murdering King Duncan (137).

### Scene 4

The old Than of Cowdor confessed and then was killed, Duncan notes that he trusted that guy.

Duncan is exited to have Macbeth lead, and announces that Malcolm will be the heir to the Scottish throne. They go to Macbeth's castle at Inverness. Macbeth tells Duncan that he is one step from becoming king, Malcolm has to give up his seat and then he is king.

### Scene 5

**Lady Macbeth** read's a letter from Macbeth telling her about the witches and the prophecy.

> Yet do I fear thy nature;
> It is too full o’ th’ milk of human kindness (16).

She does think that Macbeth can become king but is unsure if he has what it takes to fufil that role. She wants to talk to him in person out of trying to become king.

A servant enters saying that Duncan (the king) is going to stay with her. She is surprised by this, but then we get some questionable imagery of how his stay will be fatal, seemingly using her feminine traits to kill him.

She tells Macbeth and to "Look like th’ innocent flower, But be the serpent under ’t" (67).

### Scene 6

Duncan and Banquo enter, and they like the castle, Lazy Macbeth welcomes them warmly. They ask to see Macbeth.

### Scene 7

Macbeth has a whole inner monologue about whether he should kill the King and how easy it would be but how he is supposed to work for the king and that ambition is not a good reason to do it.
> Letting “I dare not” wait upon “I would,”
> Like the poor cat i’ th’ adage? (45).

He tells Lazy Macbeth that he has decided against his plan, she calls him a coward.

She sets up a new plan to kill him. They will give the servants enough wine to make them drunk, then they will kill the King and then blame it on the servants.

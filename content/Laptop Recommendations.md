# Laptop Recommendations

There are so many different use cases and price points that result in different recommendations. The goal of this document is to guide you to choosing a laptop you will enjoy based on what you are looking for in a laptop. I start with Apple laptops to get them out of the way, but feel free to skip to the [[#Windows]] section.

## MacOS

### MacBook Neo

> I want a laptop for light web browsing that just works

![macbook neo|500|center](https://files.aamirazad.com/cdn/macbook-neo-or3kgmq.webp)

No, I don’t hate Apple. The MacBook has always been the de facto high quality laptop experience, and just recently, Apple brought this experience to the broader audience with the [**MacBook Neo**](https://www.apple.com/macbook-neo). Apple is amazing at hardware: one aluminum block, killer trackpad, great keyboard, crisp little screen, all for \$600 (or \$500 if you use the student discount which I've heard is very easy to get). If macOS works for you, this is the laptop to get. But there are cons you should know if. Just know the brain inside is a slightly amped up iPhone 16 Pro chip, the RAM is locked at 8 GB forever, and Windows folks need not apply.

### MacBook Air

> I want a bit more powerful laptop

![macbook air|center](https://files.aamirazad.com/cdn/macbook-air.webp)

If you are wanting to spend a bit more to get a laptop that should last you a bit longer, with an M5 chip and 16gb of RAM, look to the [**MacBook Air**](https://www.apple.com/macbook-air). Traditionally, this was the default option for college students and the like, coming in at \$1,100. Quite a sizable jump. Still, you do get a bigger, better screen, and it will likely be more future-proof. Still, it doesn't have a fan.

### MacBook Pro

> I need all the performance

![macbook pro|center](https://files.aamirazad.com/cdn/macbook-pro.webp)

I'm not going to spend too long on this, because if you need the [**MacBook Pro**](https://www.apple.com/macbook-pro/) (\$1,700), you already know you need the MacBook Pro, and you don't need this list. Unless you have a specific use case for all the extra performance, you don't need the extra performance. This could be software development, video editing, 3D modeling, or the like. The bottom line is if you need a MacBook Pro, you will already know you need a MacBook Pro.

## Windows

### Lenovo 14" Slim 7i Aura Edition

> A good all-rounder

![lenovo-yoga-slim-aura](https://files.aamirazad.com/cdn/lenovo-yoga-slim-aura.webp-1777385321.166355-lErRvUY5.webp)

The [**Lenovo 14" Slim 7i Aura Edition**](https://www.lenovo.com/us/en/p/laptops/lenovo/lenovo_slim_series/lenovo-slim-7i-gen-10-aura-edition-14-inch-intel/len101l0050) is reasonably priced at \$1,050 and is quite good in almost every way. The CPU is mid, but not slow. It comes with 16gb of RAM which you can expect for almost every laptop at this price point right now. It is sufficient. At this moment, the market is a lot more expensive due to supply chain shortages due to AI. (Remember the [COVID chip shortage](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2020%E2%80%932023_global_chip_shortage)?, it is pretty much that again). RAM and storage is much more expensive than usual.

![memory-price-graph](https://files.aamirazad.com/cdn/memory-price-graph.webp)

Anyway, the keyboard is excellent and the OLED display paired with the strong battery life makes this my top choice for students. I recommended this laptop to Joseph, and he bought it and likes it very much. He especially likes its touchscreen display and the pen he got with it. For its price, its one of the absolute standout laptops you can buy.

### HP OmniBook X Flip 2-in-1 16"

> A good feeling laptop with some tradeoffs

![hp-omnibook](https://files.aamirazad.com/cdn/hp-omnibook.webp)

Another solid option in Windows laptop manufactures is HP. HP is known for their bang-for-the-buck laptops, although they sometimes have trade-offs. The [HP OmniBook X Flip 2-in-1 16](https://www.hp.com/us-en/shop/pdp/hp-omnibook-x-flipngai-16-as0047nr) is a great machine. Make sure to get it on sale (a state in which it is almost always in) for \$900. The CPU has good performance for most tasks while also giving solid battery life. It has a 360° touchscreen display, but the keyboard is not the best on the market. It is also 16" which means it is quite a bit bigger than most of the laptops on this list which is 14". I personally, like the smaller 14" in laptops but that is up to personal preference. If you want a smaller one, they sell the same laptop with a 14" display. The [HP OmniBook X Flip 2-in-1 14"](https://www.hp.com/us-en/shop/pdp/hp-omnibook-x-flip-14-inch-2-in-1-laptop-next-gen-ai-pc-ak4z4av-1) is a good deal at \$1000.

### Asus Zenbook 14

> A light, sleek machine with good battery life to support it

![asus zenbook](https://files.aamirazad.com/cdn/asus-zenbook.webp-1777469369.521186-lErRvUY5.webp)

I have always liked the look of the Asus Zenbooks. There are a bunch of different options, the [Asus Zenbook 14](https://www.asus.com/us/laptops/for-home/zenbook/asus-zenbook-14-oled-um3406/) (\$900) linked is not the only one, there are many different types with different CPUs in them. The [Snapdragon versions](https://www.bestbuy.com/product/asus-zenbook-a14-14-2k-oled-laptop-snapdragon-x-plus-2025-16gb-memory-512gb-storage-copilot-pc-zabriskie-beige/JJGGLH86J4/sku/12137230?ref=212&loc=marketplace) I've seen get quite cheaper and their battery life is very close to MacBook level.

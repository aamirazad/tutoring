# Laptop Recommendations

Laptops are a complicated beast. There are so many different use cases and price points that result in different recommendations. The goal of this document is to guide you to choosing a laptop you will enjoy based on what you are looking for in a laptop. I start with Apple laptops to get them out of the way, but feel free to skip to the [[#Windows| > Windows]] section.

## MacOS

## I want a laptop for light web browsing that just works

![macbook neo|500|center](https://files.aamirazad.com/cdn/macbook-neo-or3kgmq.webp)

Some people think I hate Apple. And I don't! There are many products I think are just simply the best in the space. The MacBook has always been the de facto high quality laptop experience, and just recently, apple brought this experience to the broader audience with the **[MacBook Neo](https://www.apple.com/macbook-neo)**. Apple is really good at hardware. This laptop just feels high quality. The entire laptop is milled out of a single block of aluminum, giving MacBooks their classic hefty feel to them. Also, all of the inputs that you touch are high quality. You get a nice haptic touchpad, a good looking (albeit small) display and a great keyboard. All this for $600. Or even $500 if you use the student discount (which I've heard is very easy to get). For this price nothing beats it. But there are cons you should know if. First of all, you may either be required to use Windows for your major or prefer to use Windows. I for one, cannot deal with MacOS and cannot buy a MacBook for that reason. Additionally, the CPU is the Apple A18 Pro. If you know anything about Apple's CPU this chip is the same chip as in your iPhone 16 Pro. It is slightly amped up and the phone chip is quite fast, but it will not be a speed daemon.

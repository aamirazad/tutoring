# Sonnet

14 lines, iambic pentameter. Rhymes arranged to a fixed scheme divided into a octave and a sestet.

Iambic pentameter: 5 "metric feet" each is a da-DUM (Iamb). Trochee: DUM-da (there are many others). 5 feet is pentameter. Meter is number of feet.

Stanza is a group of lines indicated by double line break (chunk) or rhyme scheme (doesn't need to be in chunks).

Rhyme scheme is marked by A, B, C, etc where A rhymes with A but not B.

Free verse without meter or rhyme scheme. Broken up by meaning, not meter or rhyme. Shakespeare Sonnet. 14 line iambic pentameter stanza broken up with certain rhyme scheme divided into three quatrains and a couplet. Rhyme scheme is ABAB, CDCD, EFEF, GG.

Italian or Petrarchan is broken up into a octave and sestet. ABBA, ABBA in the octane. In the sestet we get C, D, E ordered in different ways.

Volta is the turning point at Line 9.

The octave sets it up, creates burden, doubt, problem, vision, then the sestet address what the octave sets up, answers the question.

Enjambment is the continuation of the sense and grammatical construction onto the next verse. When the line breaks but the sentence does not stop. Could be for rhythm or to sound like what is happening in the poem

Caesura is a pause or break in a line of work. Period in the middle of a sentence for emphasis.
